## Layer B — Behavioural Analysis

**Urgency signal:** No explicit deadline, but "productionise pipelines at pace" and "contributing to hiring decisions" suggest the team is being built now and the role is open because headcount is growing, not backfilling. The automotive/IoT/payments convergence framing signals an early-stage product urgency. Frame for immediate time-to-value: show you can hire, build, and ship in parallel without waiting for the org to settle.

**Greenfield vs. fix/scale:** Greenfield. "Designing and building a modern data platform", "building infrastructure", "growing and mentoring a data engineering team" — this is a build-and-staff mandate. Lead with bullets showing platform construction and team formation from early or zero state. Avoid improvement/optimisation framing as the primary story.

**Velocity vs. rigour:** Velocity. "Productionise pipelines at pace", "collaborating with OEM partners and external developers" — speed of delivery with external partners is the primary signal. No rigour or compliance language, no data quality frameworks mentioned. Frame bullets around delivery speed, partner coordination, and shipping at scale. Avoid heavy governance language.

**Internal customer:** AI-driven pricing, payments, and financial decisioning teams — plus OEM partners and external developers as collaborators. The end beneficiaries are connected vehicle ecosystem product teams. Frame bullet outcomes in terms of enabling real-time decisioning or unblocking partner integrations, not internal analyst self-service.

**Stack lock-in:** Python is hard-required (both high-code and low-code). Microsoft ecosystem (Power BI, Azure) is hard-required — mirror both verbatim. Kafka and Spark are "strong plus" — foreground if present. RapidMiner is mentioned as a specific low-code tool — note familiarity with low-code orchestration if applicable; do not treat as hard requirement. SQL-based platforms are required; dbt not mentioned — do not foreground it.

**JD authorship:** Mixed signals — partially technical, partially recruiter-written. Technical depth indicators: "high-volume, real-time vehicle and transaction data", "medallion architectures", "AI-driven pricing, payments, and financial decisioning across connected vehicle ecosystems", "Kafka or Spark experience is a strong plus", "RapidMiner." These suggest a technical author contributed. However, the JD is unusually short for a lead role, responsibilities are described at a high level, no scale numbers are given (team size not stated, data volumes vague), and "Leadership experience or a genuine ambition to lead for the first time" is an atypically broad recruiter-friendly phrase. Implication: likely co-authored — use the specific technical terms verbatim (Kafka, medallion, real-time, Azure, Power BI) to pass ATS, but also frame leadership narrative broadly given the "ambition to lead for the first time" signal.

No current team size stated — "Not stated in JD." The role holder will both build the platform and staff the team simultaneously. Reporting line is not specified. Implication for first-90-days framing: show you can operate as a hands-on builder and a recruiter/manager in parallel — lead with examples of hiring your first data engineers while shipping production pipelines; avoid framing that assumes an existing team to delegate to.

**Domain risk:** High. The domain is "AI-driven pricing, payments, and financial decisioning across connected vehicle ecosystems" with "OEM partners" as named collaborators. Pricing and financial decisioning errors in automotive/payments contexts carry direct revenue and partner relationship consequences. "Real-time vehicle and transaction data" implies latency-sensitive pipelines where failures are immediately visible. No explicit compliance or regulatory language, but the financial decisioning framing implies accuracy requirements. Implication: frame bullets around reliability and accuracy of real-time pipelines and financial outputs — "ensured pipeline uptime for real-time pricing", "delivered accurate transaction data to OEM partners" — speed framing is acceptable but must be paired with reliability evidence.

**Collaboration width:** Moderate. Named collaborators quoted verbatim: "OEM partners", "external developers." Internal functions are not explicitly named beyond the data engineering team being built. "AI-driven pricing, payments, and financial decisioning" implies product or engineering teams as internal consumers but they are not named. Implication: narrow-to-moderate — the primary collaboration axis is external (OEM partners, external developers); lead with partner coordination and external delivery evidence rather than internal cross-functional alignment.

**Language gate:** The JD states "Professional-level German required." This is a hard gate — "required" language, not a preference or nice-to-have.
