About the role
We're looking for a highly motivated Analytics Engineer who will work closely with Product and Insurance departments to bring instant, tailored and affordable financial safety products to entrepreneurs.

What you'll be up to (some examples):
- Define and calculate KPIs for product and feature launches to track our trajectory toward 10x simplicity.
- Identify opportunities and provide data-driven recommendations that influence our roadmap.
- Answer analytical questions and analyse A/B tests to ensure we draw the right conclusions from every experiment.
- Create and maintain interactive dashboards (using Databricks and QuickSight) for real-time metrics monitoring.
- Contribute to the data transformation code base (using dbt and Git) to ensure our data infrastructure is as modern as our software.
- Own outcomes within your scope — from problem definition to delivery.
- Operate in ambiguity and make decisions without perfect information.
- Collaborate closely with colleagues across functions to bridge the gap between "the floor" and "the lab."
- Improve how we work by spotting inefficiencies in our data workflows and proposing better ways.
- Balance speed and quality in a fast-moving environment.

This role is a strong fit if you:
- Have excellent SQL skills: You can calculate any metric in a readable and efficient way.
- Are proficient with dashboarding and code tools: You have experience with tools like Databricks, QuickSight, and Git to organise your work.
- Understand the "Why": You have a strong grasp of Statistics and A/B testing to draw meaningful conclusions.
- Possess a growth mindset: You have a passion for continuous learning and seeking feedback to drive personal growth.
- Take ownership naturally: You don't wait for perfect instructions and thrive in the "grey area".
- Can turn messy problems into clear, actionable plans: You are hyper-organised and process-obsessed.

This role is not for you if:
- You are looking for a strict 40-hour work week
- You need fully defined scopes and stable priorities to perform well
- You prefer staying high-level and avoiding hands-on execution
- You expect long ramp-up periods before taking ownership
