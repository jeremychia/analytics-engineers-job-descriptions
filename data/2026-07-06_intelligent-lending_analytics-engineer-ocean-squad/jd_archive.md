Ocean Finance is a portfolio brand of Intelligent Lending Limited, a consumer credit broker powerhouse operating the TotallyMoney, Ocean Finance, CredAbility Luv Car Loans, and Binq brands. Our mission is to become the UK's leading credit platform for people not served by the mainstream — helping them make informed decisions, access the right products, and improve their financial wellbeing over their lifetime.

You'll join the Central Intelligence Unit (CIU) within our Data team. CIU sources the most relevant intelligence and ensures its optimal deployment — through machine learning, analytics and decisioning that shape product and corporate strategy. Specifically, you'll sit in the Ocean Squad, working alongside data scientists, analytics and AI engineers on the analytical frameworks, decisioning models that both elevate our product, and propel our marketing efforts for Ocean.

This is a hybrid role that sits between analytics engineering and data analysis. Roughly half your time will be spent building and maintaining the data foundations the team relies on — clean, reliable, well-modelled datasets — and the other half turning those datasets into dazzling dashboards and gleaning insights that directly inform business decisions.

It's ideal for someone early in their career (a recent graduate, up to ~2 years' experience) who enjoys both the engineering craft of making data effective as well as trustworthy, and the curiosity of digging into it to answer real commercial questions.

A day in the life of this role could look like…
- Analyzing complex datasets to uncover customer behavior patterns and product performance insights, translating these findings into clear and actionable commercial recommendations for stakeholders
- Presenting insights and recommendations to senior stakeholders, telling compelling stories with data that drive action
- Writing and optimizing SQL queries to extract and analyze data from multiple sources, ensuring accuracy and efficiency
- Collaborating with business teams to understand their analytical needs and develop solutions that drive business value
- Designing and helping to build automated dashboards and reports in tools like Power BI or Databricks to track key business metrics and support data-driven decision making
- Build and maintain data models and transformation pipelines that turn raw data into clean, well-documented, analysis-ready datasets.
- Creating and maintaining BI chatbots that leverage curated datasets to empower non-technical business stakeholders to self-serve
- Analysing market trends and competitive landscape

You'll also have the chance to get involved in other projects like:
- Evaluating the potential impact of greenfield projects, such as the integration of new products, verticals, or customer journeys
- Set up analytical framework for new products or enhance the existing ones by suggesting new KPIs / analyses
- Understanding the key drivers behind past business performance and succinctly articulating the story to the board
- Contributing to the design and implementation of our machine learning and predictive analytics initiatives

What we're looking for
- A degree in a quantitative or technical discipline (e.g. Data Science, Computer Science, Statistics, Mathematics, Economics, Engineering) — or equivalent practical experience.
- 0–2 years' experience in an analytics, data or engineering role, including internships, placements or strong personal/academic projects.
- Solid SQL skills, and confidence working with data to answer questions.
- Some exposure to a scripting language (ideally Python) for data manipulation and analysis.
- An eye for data quality and structure — you care about getting things right, not just an answer.
- Clear written and verbal communication; able to explain analysis simply.
- Curiosity and commercial awareness — you want to understand the "why" behind the numbers.
- Proactive and ownership-minded; comfortable in a fast-paced environment.

Nice to have
- Experience with a cloud data warehouse / lakehouse (e.g. Databricks, BigQuery, Snowflake, Redshift).
- Familiarity with a BI / visualisation tool (e.g. Looker, Power BI, Tableau).
- Exposure to version control (Git) and collaborative analytics workflows.
- An interest in the credit market, financial services or consumer finance.
