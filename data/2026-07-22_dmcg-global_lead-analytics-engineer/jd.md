Lead Analytics Engineer

Hybrid, London

About the company

Our client is a well known consumer technology business with a platform used by millions of people. The data team is part way through a big modernisation programme. They are rebuilding the warehouse and moving from an older ETL tool onto dbt and a modern stack.

About the role

This is a new position and the first technical lead in the data engineering team. The team is small and learning dbt as they go, so they need someone who knows it inside out and can bring everyone else up to speed. You will own the dbt models and the semantic layer, decide how things get built, and coach the rest of the team, from experienced engineers through to analysts who have never touched dbt.

Beyond the dbt work stream, you will also be sign and mend the day-to-day technical owner of the warehouse, reviewing sign and mend designs and code across the team and working closely with the analytics, data science and platform teams.

You will report to the Head of Data Engineering, who sets the overall direction. Your job is working out how to deliver it technically.

What you will be doing

· Owning the dbt models, semantic layer and warehouse design

· Leading the move away from legacy ETL onto dbt

· Setting standards for testing, documentation and CI/CD

· Coaching engineers and analysts on modern engineering practice

· Building out sign and mend data quality checks and monitoring

· Taking on the hardest technical problems when the team gets stuck

What we need from you

· Strong dbt experience covering both data engineering and analytics engineering

· Excellent SQL and solid dimensional modelling

· Hands on cloud data platform experience, ideally AWS or BigQuery

· Experience with orchestration tools like Airflow

· Experience of moving off legacy ETL platforms such as Talend

· Familiarity with tools like Fivetran and Tableau

· Clear communication. You can explain technical work in plain terms to engineers and to people across the business

· Comfortable when priorities shift, and someone who enjoys mentoring

Salary and benefits

£90,000 per year

25 days holiday plus bank holidays, rising with service

Private health and dental cover

Early finish on Fridays

Enhanced Parental Leave, pension and money towards your home office setup

Location

On site: 2 days per week, London
