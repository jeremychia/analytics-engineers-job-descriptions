The Role

We're looking for a hands-on Analytics Engineer for our next phase of growth. We're placing big bets — new products, new markets, new rails — and every one of them relies on financial reporting that keeps pace. That's where this role comes in.

You'll build and own production dbt models — spanning our core remittance business and new product launches — where what you build powers financial reporting and drives product decisions: revenue and costs modelled correctly from day one, and reconciliation that keeps our numbers trustworthy. If you love working in dbt, digging into problems from first principles, and seeing your work drive real decisions, this is the role for you.

You'll sit in our Finance Analytics team, reporting to our Finance Analytics Lead, with significant exposure to our Head of Data, directors across the company, and teams including Treasury, Product, Data Engineering and Markets.

The role is full-time – we're open to individuals in one of the following hubs: London or NYC

Key responsibilities

Own dbt models end-to-end — design, build and maintain the models in your domain, with the tests, documentation and schema coverage that keep them reliable and performant at scale

Drive product launch analytics — be the data partner to product and commercial teams at launch: commercials modelled correctly from day one, and reporting that shows how each bet is performing

Automate reconciliation at scale — build automated frameworks that prove our numbers against third-party data, across our core product and every new launch

Support finance and compliance reporting — deliver the datasets behind month-end close and audit requests, and build the pipelines that make each cycle faster

Use AI effectively — apply AI tools and agents across the modelling lifecycle, from accelerating dbt development to improving data quality

Contribute to our analytics community — peer-review code, share knowledge at weekly meetups, and rotate on the analytics first-responder rota answering data questions from across the business

You have
